Chicken & Chorizo Risotto

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is a much-loved family classic in the UK. Made with rich beef mince simmered in a tomato-based sauce, it’s hearty, comforting, and full of flavour. Perfect for a weeknight dinner or a Sunday family meal, this version strikes a balance between traditional Italian inspiration and British home-cooking style.

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  1. 500g Beef Mince
  2. 1 tablespoon olive oil
  3. 1 onion, finely chopped
  4. 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  5. 1 medium carrot, grated or finely diced
  6. 2 table spoons toamto purée
  7. 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
  8. 100ml beef stock
  9. 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  10. 1 teaspoon dried basil
  11. 1 tablespoon Worcestershire Sauce
  12. Salet and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  13. 300-400g dried spaghetti
  14. Grated Parmesan cheese, to serve (optional)
  15. Fresh parsley, chopped, to garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan or saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook gently for 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and carrot, cooking for a further 2–3 minutes.
  3. Increase the heat slightly and add the beef mince. Cook until browned all over, breaking it up with a wooden spoon as it cooks.
  4. Stir in the tomato purée and cook for a minute before adding the chopped tomatoes, beef stock, oregano, basil, and Worcestershire sauce.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, bring to the boil, then reduce the heat. Simmer gently for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  6. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti in a large pan of salted boiling water according to the packet instructions. Drain and set aside.
  7. Taste the Bolognese and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  8. Serve the Bolognese over the cooked spaghetti, topped with grated Parmesan and a sprinkle of chopped parsley if you like.
